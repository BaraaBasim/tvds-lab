About the group

A research group built around the dimension of time.

The Time-Varying Data Science (TVDS) Group studies how to reason about data whose structure and statistics are non-stationary — changing as time passes and as the relationships between entities evolve. Led by Prof. Ercan E. Kuruoglu, we bring together statistical signal processing, Bayesian learning, and graph machine learning to build models that are robust, interpretable, and aware of uncertainty.

Our methods are applied to climate, financial, genomic, and remote-sensing data, where understanding change is the whole point.

Research directions

What we work on

Methodological depth across statistics, signal processing, and machine learning — unified by the question of how things change.

Bayesian Learning

Probabilistic models that quantify uncertainty and adapt as new data arrives — from inference to sequential decision-making.

Graph Signal Processing

Processing and predicting signals defined on the nodes and edges of networks, including time-varying and dynamic topologies.

Statistical Signal Processing

Non-Gaussian and impulsive data, stable distributions, and robust adaptive algorithms for real-world noisy signals.

Topological Data Analysis

Capturing the shape of high-dimensional data with simplicial and topological methods for statistical learning.

Stochastic Deep Learning

Dynamic and statistical deep-learning approaches for non-deterministic, non-stationary systems.

Applications

Climate, financial, genomic, and remote-sensing data — where modelling change drives real scientific impact.
