Biography

Ercan Engin Kuruoglu is a Full Professor of Data Science at Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School, affiliated with the Institute of Data and Information and the Tsinghua-Berkeley Shenzhen Institute. He earned his Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge and is a Fellow of the IEEE.

Before joining Tsinghua, he was a Chief Scientist (Dirigente di Ricerca) at the Italian National Research Council (CNR) in Pisa. He first came to Shenzhen in 2016 and relocated permanently in 2019. He is ranked among the top 2% of the world's most-cited scientists and serves as Editor-in-Chief of Digital Signal Processing (Elsevier).

Research interests

His work spans statistical signal processing, Bayesian learning, stable distributions and non-Gaussian data analysis, graph signal processing, and topological data analysis. A unifying theme is the modelling of time-varying and non-stationary phenomena, with applications in computational biology, geosciences, remote sensing, and climate, financial, and genomic data.

Studying & opportunities at the lab

The group regularly recruits PhD students, postdoctoral researchers, and visiting scholars. We look for candidates with strong mathematical foundations and a genuine interest in statistical and probabilistic methods. Backgrounds in electrical/information engineering, statistics, applied mathematics, or computer science are all welcome.

Postdoctoral — Topological Data Analysis for Statistical Learning

Developing topological signal processing and statistical learning methods for high-dimensional data, with applications to climate, financial, and genomic datasets.

Postdoctoral — Stochastic Deep Learning

Developing dynamic and statistical deep-learning approaches for non-deterministic and non-stationary systems.

Typical qualifications: a Ph.D. (or, for PhD applicants, a strong Master's/Bachelor's) in electrical/electronics/information engineering with a specialization in signal processing or statistical learning, or in mathematics (topology, statistics, functional analysis), together with a track record of high-quality publications.

How to apply: send your CV, a one-page personal statement, two referee contacts, transcripts, and representative publications to kuruoglu@sz.tsinghua.edu.cn.
